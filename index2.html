Timebook – hackathon, social networks, dbpedia, art, museums

Timebook is a social network of historical figures built from Linked Open Data found on DBPedia, Europeana and WikiQuote. The main question we want to answer is: what if social media existed before? can it be used to explore, learn and understand art movements and collections?

Joint work with Luca Chiarandini. Code available at github. Winners of the Social Impact category at Europeana's Hack4Europe hackathon in Barcelona.

UN Global Voices – united nations, global voices, visualization

I participated on a visualization contest about United Nations data from the Voices of the Vulnerable program. The visualization tries to resume more than 500 answers in natural language to questions about how the people feel about their current economic situations and their future. Our work did not win but it received good feedback. Joint work with Ruth Garcia Gavilanes from Yahoo/UPF.

The Visualization submitted by Eduardo Graells Garrido raises the critical question of the complexity of classifying natural language with grammar errors and ambiguity. It shows a very interesting graph representation that classifies the topics related to changes in the last year (Q3).

See the visualization here. It has been features on the Impact Blog of the London School of Economics and Political Science.

Visualization of #20N – elections, twitter, visualization

One of the first projects I did on the PhD. I implemented a real-time visualization that displayed how Twitter reacted to the on-going presidential elections in Spain in November 20th, 2011. It was one of my first experiments with django + d3.js.

Transantiago - Corridor Speeds – maps, public transport, transantiago

I worked in the Department of Civil Engineering, Transport Division at University of Chile, under the supervision of Marcela Munizaga. My main task was to develop tools to analyze corridor speeds in the Transantiago public transport system. Corridor speeds were estimated according to GPS information from all vehicles on the system.

Chileclic - Search Engine for the Chilean Government – search, government

Chileclic was a search engine from the government developed at Center for Web Research . I designed and implemented the search user interface, which needed to be embedded into the original design of the site. The development included a back-end dashboard of query analytics.
